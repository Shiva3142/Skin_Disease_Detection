    (1)Acne:Acne is a very common skin condition that causes pimples mostly on the face, 
    forehead, chest, shoulders and upper back. 
    80% of people ages 11 to 30 will have at least a mild form of acne, and most people are 
    affected by it at some point in their lives.

Causes:1)Fluctuating hormone levels around the time of a woman’s period.
    2)Picking at acne sores.
    3)Clothing and headgear, like hats and sports helmets.
    4)Air pollution and certain weather conditions, especially high humidity.
    5)Using oily or greasy personal care products,Stress which increases the hormone cortisol, 
    can also cause acne to flare.
    6)Some medications.
    7)Genetics.

Cure:1)For mild:Benzoyl peroxide- This ingredient helps dry out existing pimples, prevents new 
ones from forming, and kills acne-causing bacteria.
             Salicylic acid- This ingredient helps exfoliate your skin to prevent pores from 
             getting clogged with acne-causing bacteria
  2)For moderate:prescription-strength benzoyl peroxide
                 antibiotics, like erythromycin or clindamycin
                 retinoids, such as retinol
  3)For Severe:1)oral antibiotics
               2)benzoyl peroxide
               3)topical antibiotics
               4)topical retinoids

Home Remedies:1)Apply aloe vera.
           2)Use a honey mask.

Prevention:1)Wash your face daily with warm water and a mild facial cleanser.
        2)Routinely use moisturizer.
        3)Keep your hands away from your face.
       

(2)Rosacea:Rosacea is a common skin condition that causes blushing or flushing and 
visible blood 
vessels in your face. 
        It may also produce small, pus-filled bumps. These signs and symptoms may flare 
        up for weeks to months and then go away for a while.

Causes:1)Rosacea often runs in families.
    2)Blood vessel trouble- The redness on your skin might be due to problems with 
    blood vessels in your face. 
      Sun damage could cause them to get wider, which makes it easier for other people 
      to see them.
    3)Mites- They're tiny insects. A type called Demodex folliculorum normally lives on 
    your skin and usually isn't harmful. 
      Some people, though, have a heightened sensitivity to the mites, or more of these 
      bugs than usual.Too many mites could irritate your skin.
    4)eating spicy foods,eating items that contain the compound cinnamaldehyde, such as 
    cinnamon, chocolate, tomatoes, and citrus

Cure:1)Brimonidine (Mirvaso), a gel that tightens blood vessels in the skin to get rid 
of some of your redness.
  2)Azelaic acid, a gel and foam that clears up bumps, swelling, and redness.

Prevention:1)Avoid rubbing or touching the face.
        2)Wash the face with a gentle cleanser, and avoid using products that contain 
        irritants or alcohol.
        3)Avoid comedogenic products. These block the oil and sweat gland openings.
        4)Keep the skin hydrated with a gentle moisturizer. Wait for topical 
        medications to dry before moisturizing.
        5)Avoid extreme temperatures.
        6)Avoid foods and beverages that might trigger an outbreak.

(3)Atopic Dematitis:Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is a condition that makes your skin red 
and itchy. 
                 It's common in children but can occur at any age. Atopic dermatitis is 
                 long lasting (chronic) and tends to flare periodically. 
                 It may be accompanied by asthma or hay fever.

itchiness.They come in forms such as:
  1)gels

(4)Actinic Keratosis:An actinic keratosis is a rough, scaly patch on the skin that 
develops from years of sun exposure. 
                  It's often found on the face, lips, ears, forearms, scalp, neck or 
                  back of the hands.

Causes:An actinic keratosis is caused by frequent or intense exposure to UV rays from 
the sun or tanning beds.

Cure:1)Freezing (cryotherapy). 
  2)Scraping (curettage). 
  3)Laser therapy. 
  4)Photodynamic therapy. 

Prevention:1)Limit your time in sun.
        2)Use sunscreen.
        3)Avoid tanning beds.

(5)Bullous Disease:Bullous pemphigoid (BUL-us PEM-fih-goid) is a rare skin condition 
that causes large, fluid-filled blisters. 
                They develop on areas of skin that often flex — such as the lower 
                abdomen, upper thighs or armpits. Bullous pemphigoid is most common in 
                older adults.

Causes:Bullous pemphigoid is an autoimmune disorder. That means it occurs when the 
body’s own immune system attacks the layer of tissue below the top layer of skin. 
    The reasons for this attack are not known, but bullous pemphigoid can sometimes be 
    triggered by certain medications, including penicillamine.

Cure:Bullous pemphigoid is most commonly treated with corticosteroids. 
  These can be taken by mouth in addition to topically applied creams. 
  Antibiotics or other anti-inflammatory medications may also be useful in mild cases. 
  Severe cases may require the use of immunosuppressant medications.

Prevention:1)Avoid sun exposure. 
        2)Avoid prolonged sun exposure on any area of the skin affected by bullous 
        pemphigoid. 
        3)Dress in loosefitting cotton clothes. This helps protect your skin.

(6)Cellulitis Impetigo:Impetigo is a common and highly contagious skin infection that 
mainly affects infants and young children. 
           It usually appears as reddish sores on the face, especially around the nose 
           and mouth and on the hands and feet. 
           Over about a week, the sores burst and develop honey-colored crusts.

Causes:Impetigo is caused by bacteria, usually staphylococci organisms.

Cure:Impetigo is treated with prescription mupirocin antibiotic ointment or cream 
applied directly to the sores two to three times a day for five to 10 days.

Prevention:Keeping skin clean is the best way to keep it healthy. It's important to 
wash cuts, scrapes, insect bites and other wounds right away.

(7)Eczema:Eczema is a condition in which patches of skin become inflamed, itchy, 
cracked, and rough. Some types can also cause blisters.

Causes:It develops from a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

Cure:1)Topical corticosteroid creams and ointments: 
 2)Oral medications
 3)Antibiotics
 4)Phototherapy

Prevention:1)Applying moisturizer within 3 minutes of bathing to “lock in” moisture
        2)moisturizing every day
        3)wearing cotton and soft fabrics    
        4)avoiding rough, scratchy fibers and tight fitting clothing
        5)using a humidifier in dry or cold weather

(8) Exanthem:An exanthem is a rash or eruption on the skin. 
         "Viral" means that the rash or eruption is a symptom of an infection due to a 
         virus. 
          Viral exanthems can be caused by many viruses, such as enteroviruses, 
          adenovirus, chickenpox, measles, rubella, mononucleosis, and certain types of 
          herpes infection.

Causes:Exanthems are usually caused by viral or bacterial infections. They can also be 
caused by drugs, toxins, or autoimmune diseases. 

Cure:1)Antibiotics for treating bacterial infections. 
  2)Calamine lotion (to relieve itching) Antiviral drugs (for severe cases) Rest.

Prevention:1)Avoid scrubbing your skin.
        2)Use gentle cleansers.
        3)Avoid applying cosmetic lotions or ointments directly on the rash.
        4)Use warm (not hot) water for cleaning. ...
        5)Leave the affected area exposed to the air as much as possible.

(9)Alopecia:Alopecia areata is a common autoimmune disorder that often results in 
unpredictable hair loss.

Causes:The condition occurs when white blood cells attack the cells in hair follicles, 
causing them to shrink and dramatically slow down hair production.

Cure:use of corticosteroids, powerful anti-inflammatory drugs that can suppress the 
immune system. 
  These are mostly commonly administered through local injections, topical ointment 
  application, or orally.

Prevention:1)Avoid hairstyles that pull on the hair.
        2)Avoid high-heat hair styling tools.
        3)Don’t chemically treat or bleach your hair.
        Use a shampoo that’s mild and suited for your hair.

(10)Herpes:herpes is common sexually transmitted infections that can affect a person’s 
skin.

Causes:Herpes infections are caused by herpes simplex virus (HSV) types 1 and 2. These 
viruses are contagious and transmitted from one person to another through skin-to-skin 
contact. 
           Kissing or touching is the main cause of HSV-1 transmission, and sexual 
           contact is the main cause of HSV-2 transmission. 

Cure:a person can take medication to shorten outbreaks reduce their frequency

Prevention:1)wearing loose clothing can help reduce irritation from blisters
        2)keeping skin clean and dry supports the healing of sores
        3)taking anti-herpes medication every day
        4)avoiding sexual contact during outbreaks

(11)HPV:HPV is common sexually transmitted infections that can affect a person’s skin.

Causes:By having vaginal, anal, or oral sex with someone who has the virus. 
    It is most commonly spread during vaginal or anal sex. It also spreads through 
    close skin-to-skin touching during sex. 

Cure:medication for genital warts, health checks for high-risk HPV	

Prevention:1)using a condom or dental dam during every sexual encounter, including oral 
sex.

(12)Lupus:Lupus is a disease that occurs when your body's immune system attacks your 
own tissues and organs (autoimmune disease). 
       Inflammation caused by lupus can affect many different body systems — including 
       your joints, skin, kidneys, blood cells, brain, heart and lungs,

Causes:1)Lupus is an autoimmune condition, but the exact cause is unclear.
    2)There may be a genetic factor to lupus. If you have family members who have 
    lupus, your risk of the disease is increased.

Cure:Common treatments for lupus include:
  1)Hydroxychloroquine: This is an antimalarial drug that treats rashes and arthritis 
  symptoms in lupus.
  2)Belimumab: This is a biological agent approved by the Food and Drug Administration 
  (FDA) to treat SLE(Systemic lupus erythematosus).

Prevention:1)Avoiding sun exposure.
        2)Staying in motion like do exercise.
        3)Maintaining healthy habits

          Melanoma can also form in your eyes and, rarely, inside your body, such as in 
          your nose or throat.

Causes:Melanoma occurs when something goes wrong in the melanin-producing cells 
(melanocytes) that give color to your skin. eg(DNA Damage).

Cure:1)Surgery to remove affected lymph nodes. 
  2)Immunotherapy
  3)Targeted therapy 
  4)Radiation therapy 
  5)Chemotherapy

Prevention:1)Avoid the sun during the middle of the day. 
        2)Wear sunscreen  
        3)Wear protective clothing. 
        4)Avoid tanning lamps and beds. Tanning lamps and beds emit UV rays and can 
        increase your risk of skin cancer.
        5)Become familiar with your skin so that you'll notice changes.

have hair growing out of them. 
       They are generally round or oval, and have a smooth edge.

Causes:1)when cells in the skin called melanocytes grow in clusters. 

Cure:1)The majority of moles are harmless and require no treatment.

(15)Nail Fungus:Nail fungus is a common condition that begins as a white or yellow spot 
under the tip of your fingernail or toenail. 
             As the fungal infection goes deeper, nail fungus may cause your nail to 
             discolor, thicken and crumble at the edge. It can affect several nails.

Causes:1)Fungal nail infections are caused by various fungal organisms (fungi). The 
most common cause is a type of fungus called dermatophyte. 
    2)Yeast and molds also can cause nail infections.

Cure:1)Oral antifungal drugs like terbinafine (Lamisil) and itraconazole (Sporanox).
  2)Medicated nail polish. Your doctor may prescribe an antifungal nail polish called 
  ciclopirox (Penlac). 
  3)Medicated nail cream. Your doctor may prescribe an antifungal cream.

Prevention:1)Wash your hands and feet regularly. Wash your hands after touching an 
infected nail. Moisturize your nails after washing.
        2)Trim nails straight across, smooth the edges with a file and file down 
        thickened areas. Disinfect your nail clippers after each use.
        3)Wear sweat-absorbing socks or change your socks throughout the day.
        4)Choose shoes made of materials that breathe.

                                The rash isn't contagious or life-threatening, but it

Causes:Contact dermatitis is caused by a substance you're exposed to that irritates

(17)Psoriasis and Lichen Planus: lichen planus and psoriasis can cause patches of scaly 
bumps or rashes on the skin. 
                              Lichen planus is a common inflammatory skin condition. It 
                              is not contagious and not a sign of cancer. It appears as 
                              firm, shiny, reddish bumps.
                              Psoriasis is the most common autoimmune condition in the 
                              United States. It is also not contagious or a sign of 
                              cancer.
Causes:1)Psoriasis is an autoimmune condition.
    2)The cause of lichen planus is usually not known, although possible causes 
    include: Hepatitis C, a virus that attacks your liver. 
      Certain medicines, including some drugs used to treat high blood pressure, 
      diabetes, heart disease, and malaria. Reactions to metal fillings in your teeth.

Cure:1)Lichen planus does not usually require treatment. It often goes away by itself 
within a year.
    If a person has particularly itchy or painful outbreaks, a doctor may prescribe 
    topical corticosteroids or light therapy.
  2)For psoriasis: a)topical treatments
                   b)oral treatments
                   c)light therapy
                   d)systemic treatments, which are drugs that work throughout the body
                   e)biologic drug therapy

Prevention:1)For lichen planus:Avoid injuries to your skin. Apply cool compresses 
instead of scratching. Limit the stress in your life.
        2)For Psoriasis:a)Use Moisturizing Lotions
                        b)Take Care of Your Skin and Scalp
                        c)Avoid Dry, Cold Weather
                        d)Use a Humidifier
                        e)Avoid Medications That Cause Flare-Ups

(18)Scabies and Lyme Disease :Scabies is an itchy skin condition caused by a tiny 
burrowing mite called Sarcoptes scabiei. Intense itching occurs in the area where the 
mite burrows.
                           Lyme disease is transmitted by the bite of an infected 
                           black-legged tick, commonly known as a deer tick.

Causes:1)Human scabies is caused by an infestation of the skin by the human itch mite 
(Sarcoptes scabiei var. hominis)
    2)Lyme disease is caused by the bacteria Borrelia burgdorferi and Borrelia mayonii, 
    carried primarily by black-legged or deer ticks. 

Cure:1)Several creams and lotions are available with a doctor's prescription.
  2)Antibiotics are used to treat Lyme disease.

Prevention:1)Cover up. When in wooded or grassy areas, wear shoes, long pants tucked 
into your socks, a long-sleeved shirt, a hat and gloves. 
        2)Use insect repellents.
        3)Do your best to tick-proof your yard. Clear brush and leaves where ticks 
        live. 
        4)Check your clothing, yourself, your children and your pets for ticks.

(19)seborrheic keratosis and benign tumor: A seborrheic keratosis is a common 
noncancerous (benign) skin growth. People tend to get more of them as they get older.
                                        Seborrheic keratoses are usually brown, black 
                                        or light tan. The growths (lesions) look waxy 
                                        or scaly and slightly raised. 
                                        Benign tumors are noncancerous growths in the 
                                        body. Unlike cancerous tumors, they don’t 
                                        spread (metastasize) to other parts of the 
                                        body. 

Causes:1)Experts don't completely understand what causes a seborrheic keratosis. This 
type of skin growth does tend to run in families, so there is likely an inherited 
tendency.
    2)The exact cause of a benign tumor is often unknown. It develops when cells in the 
    body divide and grow at an excessive rate.

Cure:1)A seborrheic keratosis typically doesn't go away on its own, but treatment isn't 
needed. You might choose to have it removed if it becomes irritated or bleeds
  2)For benign tumor: Medicated gels or creams may help reduce some tumors, such as 
  hemangiomas.

Prevention:1)There is no way to completely prevent the development of seborrheic 
keratoses.
        2)Surgery is a common type of treatment for benign tumors.

(22)Urticaria Hives:Hives (urticaria) are red, itchy welts that result from a skin 
reaction. The welts vary in size and appear and fade repeatedly as the reaction runs 
its course.
                 The condition is considered chronic hives if the welts appear for more 
                 than six weeks and recur frequently over months or years. 

Causes:The welts that come with hives arise when certain cells release histamine and 
other chemicals into your bloodstream.

Cure:1)Antihistamines
  2)Anti-inflammation medications

Prevention:1)Avoid scratching or rubbing the skin.
        2)Avoid being in high-humidity areas or wearing tight clothing if you have 
        recently had a hives outbreak.

(24)Vasculitis: It involves inflammation of the blood vessels. 
             The inflammation can cause the walls of the blood vessels to thicken, 
             which reduces the width of the passageway through the vessel. 
             If blood flow is restricted, it can result in organ and tissue damage.

Causes: It happens when the body's immune system attacks the blood vessel by mistake. 
It can happen because of an infection, a medicine, or another disease.

Cure:A corticosteroid drug, such as prednisone, is the most common type of drug
